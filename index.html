The cost of an ANU degree before/after deregulation

Deregulation

The Abbott government has recently proposed changes to the higher education system which will affect both the tuition fees charged by the ANU as well as the interest rate applied to student loans. In the current system, tuition fees are set at a fixed rate depending on the degree and there is no interest applied to student loans. In the proposed system, tuition fees are no longer fixed and universities may charge any rate as long as it is below the international rate. In addition, student loans will be subject to an interest rate which is pegged to the ten-year treasury bond rate.

You may compare the cost of an education at the ANU before and after deregulation by choosing values for each of the relevant variables.

Degree length

Choose here the number of years it takes to complete the degree. A typical ANU degree takes 3 to 4 years.

Tuition fees

In the deregulated system, domestic tuition fees will increase on average in order to compensate for the 20% decrease in commonwealth funding. It is unclear though exactly by how much tuition fees will increase. In our model, you may choose yourself the domestic tuition fees.

Depending on the subject area, we have set the minimum domestic tuition fee as the tuition fees that would be necessary to compensate for the change in government funding, and we have set the maximum fees as the difference between the current international rates and the commonwealth contribution. The default is set as the average of this minimum and maximum.

We note that while the domestic tuition fees are capped by the difference between the current international rate and the commonwealth contribution, there is a possibility that the administration may raise the international tuition fees, in which case the total cost of an education would be higher than predicted by this model.

In medicine, the necessary tuition fees to compensate for the change in government funding ($14.3 K) is actually more than the difference between the current international fees ($30 K) and the commonwealth contribution ($18K). Therefore, we allowed for the possibility of a 20% increase in international tuition fees.

Inflation Rate

In the current regulated system, student debt is indexed by the inflation rate. The default value in this model is set as 2.77% which is the 10-year average of the quarterly inflation rates. Interest Rate

Under the proposed deregulated system, student debt will accumulate interest. This interest rate is determined by the 10-year treasury bond rate. The interest rate will be capped at 6% if the 10-year treasury bond rate exceeds 6%. The default value in this model is set as 5.0% which is the 10-year average of the monthly 10-year treasury bond rates. The current 10-year treasury bond rate (as of 7 June, 2014) is 3.77%. The following graphic illustrates how the 10-year treasury bond rate has varied since 1969 with the red line indicating 6%.

Gap years

Choose here the number of years that elapse before the student begins earning the specified starting salary. These gap years could represent years pursuing a graduate degree, years dedicated to volunteer work, or years taken to raise a family.

Starting salary

Choose here a starting salary. This represents the annual salary that the student will make in their first job after uni.

This figure should be entered in today's dollars. This means that if you choose $50,000 as a starting salary and you begin earning this salary in the year 2024, you will actually be earning 50000*(1+inflation)^10 dollars. For instance, if the inflation rate is 2.77%, your actual starting salary will be $65,710 in 2024 dollars.

Annual raises as percentage

Choose here an annual raise given as a percentage. For instance, if you begin earning $50000 in the year 2020 and this annual raise percentage is 5%, then your salary in 2021 is $52500, in 2022 is $55125, and so on.

Remaining debt

This model assumes that the student retires 50 years after graduating from the ANU. This number reflects the amount of debt remaining on the loan when the student retires. It is shown in red in the graph.
